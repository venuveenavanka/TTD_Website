About Our Temple

History & Significance

Thirupathe Devathanam Temple is an ancient spiritual sanctuary dedicated to Lord Vishnu, located in the sacred town of Thirupathe. The temple dates back over 1000 years and is one of the 108 Divya Desams mentioned in the Vaishnava canon.

The temple architecture showcases the Dravidian style with intricate carvings and sculptures that depict scenes from Hindu mythology. The temple complex includes several mandapams, shrines, and sacred tanks that have been restored to their former glory.

Devotees visit Thirupathe Devathanam to seek blessings from the presiding deity and to experience the divine atmosphere that pervades the temple premises. The temple follows ancient rituals and traditions that have been preserved for centuries.

Temple Features

Daily Pujas

Multiple pujas are performed daily according to the Agama shastras, including early morning, noon, and evening rituals.

Festivals

Annual festivals like Brahmotsavam, Vaikunta Ekadashi, and other special occasions are celebrated with grandeur.

Annadanam

Free food is served to all devotees daily as part of our service to humanity and devotion to the Lord.

Ancient Architecture

The temple showcases magnificent Dravidian architecture with intricate carvings and majestic gopurams.
